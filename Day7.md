# My Claude Usage Strategy

## Recommended Primary Model
**Claude Sonnet 4.6**, with **Opus** as an occasional upgrade for genuinely hard problems.

## Why This Model Fits Me
You're a student doing daily coding, learning, and resume/career prep, with a strong preference for fast answers plus coding help. That's high-frequency, moderate-complexity work — exactly where Sonnet 4.6 shines: smart and fast enough for daily use, without the slower pace and higher cost of Opus. Saving Opus for the genuinely tough stuff keeps your usage sustainable as a daily user.

## When to Use Haiku
Haiku 4.5 is your speed tool for low-stakes, high-volume tasks: quick syntax lookups, formatting a resume bullet, summarizing a short article, rewording an email, or any "just give me the answer fast" moment. Use it when you already know roughly what you want and just need execution speed.

## When to Use Sonnet
Your default for almost everything: writing and debugging code, explaining concepts while learning, building/refining your resume and cover letters, mock interview prep, study guides, and multi-step homework or project help. It balances quality and speed well enough for daily heavy use.

## When to Use Opus
Reserve for: hard algorithmic/debugging problems where Sonnet keeps missing the bug, multi-file architecture decisions, deep conceptual explanations in a tough course (e.g., a confusing proof or system design topic), and high-stakes career documents (final resume polish, personal statement for an internship/grad application) where you want the most careful pass.

## Recommended Effort Level

### Low
Quick lookups, syntax reminders, one-line fixes, simple definitions while studying.

### Standard (this is "High" in claude.ai, the default)
Your everyday setting — most coding help, learning explanations, resume drafts, homework support. Leave it here most of the time.

### High (claude.ai's "Extra"/xhigh)
Multi-file coding tasks, debugging something tricky, a research-heavy assignment, or refining a resume/cover letter for a specific job.

### Max
Reserved for the few high-stakes moments: a genuinely hard bug you've been stuck on, a critical application essay, or a complex project with real consequences if it's wrong.

## My Personalized Claude Workflow

| Task | Best Model | Best Effort | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quick code syntax/error lookup | Haiku | Low | Fast, low-stakes, you just need the answer |
| Writing/debugging code (daily) | Sonnet | Standard | Good balance of reasoning and speed |
| Hard bug / multi-file refactor | Opus | High or Max | Needs deeper reasoning across context |
| Learning a new concept | Sonnet | Standard | Explains clearly without overkill |
| Confusing/advanced topic | Opus | High | Needs careful, layered explanation |
| Resume bullet polishing | Haiku | Low | Quick rewrite, no deep reasoning needed |
| Full resume/cover letter draft | Sonnet | High | Needs structure, tone, and tailoring |
| Final resume for dream job/internship | Opus | Max | High stakes, worth the extra quality |
| Mock interview practice | Sonnet | Standard | Conversational, iterative, fast enough |
| Daily quick questions | Haiku | Low | Speed over depth |

## Biggest Mistakes I Should Avoid
Don't default to Opus or Max effort for everyday questions — it burns through your usage faster and isn't needed for routine coding or studying. Conversely, don't stay on Low effort for genuinely hard debugging or important documents; you'll get shallow answers that miss edge cases. Also avoid treating "Max effort" as a magic fix — it makes Claude try harder, not necessarily get it right, so a vague prompt at Max still gets you a thorough answer to the wrong question. Write clear prompts first, then dial up effort.

## Final Recommendation
If you could only pick one combo: **Sonnet 4.6 at Standard (High) effort** — the default in claude.ai. It comfortably handles coding, learning, and resume work without wasting your daily limits, and you can manually bump to Opus or Max effort only for the rare hard problem or high-stakes document.

- Claude is excellent for writing, documentation, and explanations.
